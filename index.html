Websites

techne||theos
A speculative exploration of a post-secular, post-biological future where technology and spirituality converge. In this world, DNA, data, and AI become new forms of sacred texts and divine forces. The project examines how hybrid rituals (blending the biological, digital, and mythic) transform humanity's eternal search for meaning, transcendence, and moral guidance in an age where entities of divinity circulate as code.

overcast.exe
A React-based collection of cloud photographs captured across New York City at different times of day. The interface uses temporal navigation between three intervals, each with its own visual atmosphere created through shifting background colors and evolving ASCII art that mirror the ephemeral qualities of daylight. The project pairs documentary photography with environmental design to invite viewers to notice the overlooked beauty in daily atmospheric changes above the city.

Finance and Cigarette Culture commentary
A web-based interface design that recreates a Windows 11 desktop environment to examine the material culture of financial work. The desktop functions as an interactive system where each icon opens a curated window into hedge fund analyst life. The design appropriates familiar desktop conventions to reframe the cigarette break, captured through photography and spatial observation, as a designed moment of resistance within the digital infrastructure of finance capital.

Seen at 2:14 AM... remembered for a lifetime
This project transforms 659 Instagram messages into nine poems, bridging raw JSON data with intimate human connection. The design emphasizes the contrast between code's clinical structure and emotional memory, turning timestamps and metadata into a visual meditation on how digital traces carry profound weight. Each poem reveals a self-portrait built from conversation's architecture, making the invisible layers of digital life tangible and resonant.

About.txt

Hello!

I’m Isabella– Isa for short! A *self-proclaimed* creative technologist, interdisciplinary designer, and multimedia artist.

Originally from the Bay Area, I am currently based in New York City, where I study at Parsons School of Design. I’m majoring in [Design and Technology], and minoring in [Communication Design] and [Society, Technology, and Management].

My practice lives at the intersection of visual systems and experiential design, bridging digital interfaces with physical environments, strategic thinking with artistic exploration. I approach design as both a craft and a conversation, using computational tools and tactile materials to investigate fundamental questions of human experience.

Through web design, interactive systems, and visual communication, I create work that examines our evolving relationship with technology. My process integrates design strategy with hands-on making, treating every project as an opportunity to reconsider how we navigate digital and physical spaces.

Whether designing for screens or surfaces, interfaces or installations, I'm interested in the conceptual space where algorithms meet handicraft. My work invites reflection on what it means to design—to be human—in an increasingly technological world.
